Water Density In Dependence On Temperature

This formular needs to activate JavaScript on your webbrowser.

The methode for the water density calculation according to PAUL and to CHEN and MILLEROW (considered to be the most exact one) can be used only for the limnological range. Seawater density can be calculated according to FOFOLOFF and MILLARD (specific volume = 1/density).
While using this formular please keep in mind there is no guarantee about the content e.g. for the calculation results. Further information much more detailed you will get out of the original literature.


INPUT

(1) water temperature Tc (0...30°C)=  °C
(2) pressure (0...180 bar)=  bar
(3) salinity (0...0,6 PSS-78)=  PSS-78

    Tc = water temperature in °C
    T  = water temperature in Kelvin (= Tc + 273.15)





RESULTS

(1) water density = f{T}:  g/l (simple method according to Paul ´85)
(2) water density = f{p;S;T}:  g/l (Chen and Millero)
(3) water density = f{p;S;T}:  g/l (Fofonoff and Millard)



Formulars In Use:

(1) rho = D(j)
    D = a(j) + D*T {j from 4 to 0}
    a(0) = 999.84102229
    a(1) = 0.067477741278
    a(2) = -0.0089764830531
    a(3) = 0.000088534831535
    a(4) = -6.5515551052E-07
(2) rho = rho0 * (1 - p / K) ^ -1
    rho0 = density desalinated water in g/cm3 rho0 = 0.9998395 + 0.000067914 * T - 0.0000090894 * T * T +0.00000010171 * T * T * T - 0.0000000012846 * T ^ 4 + 0.000000000011592 * T ^ 5 - 5.0125E-14 * T ^ 6 + (0.0008181-0.00000385 * T + 0.0000000496 * T * T) * S
    K =19652.17 + 148.113 * T * -2.293 * T * T + 0.01256 * T ^ 3 -0.0000418 * T ^ 4 + (3.2726 - 0.0002147 * T + 0.0001128 * T ^ 2) * p + (53.238 - 0.313 * T + 0.005728 * p) * S
(3) rho = 1 / ((1 - p/K) /R)
    R and K are underliing various calculations with a huge number of determined values describing the effects of pressure, salinity and temperature.
